# IGOTIN Content Lens

Use this reference before clustering, topic selection, and production prioritization.

## Winning Patterns

Recent `@igotin.official` insights indicate that the strongest content is not generic education news. It usually has one of these properties:

- It gives students a reason to send it to a friend.
- It names a specific school, class year, identity, situation, or anxiety.
- It creates surprise through a weird course, rule, cost, ranking, or campus culture detail.
- It turns a dry update into "what this means for your choice, money, housing, or social life."
- It invites disagreement without becoming irresponsible or sensational.

## Topic Lanes

Use these lanes in signals, clusters, rankings, and report tables when the MCP schema allows.

| Lane | Why it matters | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| `unexpected_culture` | Surprising college culture/course/rule stories can drive reach, shares, and follows. | classes you would not believe exist, campus rules that sound fake |
| `identity_belonging` | Identity, safety, belonging, and community-choice content tends to be shareable. | first-gen, international, LGBTQ+, transfer, commuter students |
| `school_specific_utility` | School/year-specific content can produce comments and follow intent. | Class of 2030 checklist, move-in by school |
| `freshman_transition` | Dorm, roommate, orientation, and first-week anxiety create practical share reasons. | roommate red flags, dorm packing by climate |
| `cost_anxiety` | Tuition, rent, meals, hidden fees, and city costs create reach and save reasons. | meal plan math, rent near campus, hidden fees |
| `debate_ranking` | Rankings can create reach, but need a comment prompt to avoid passive viewing. | overrated traditions, safest/most expensive campuses |
| `career_ai` | Majors, internships, jobs, and AI work when tied to decisions. | major regret + internship outcomes, AI tools students actually use |
| `international_student` | Visa, cost, school choice, and adaptation are strong when segment-specific. | US vs Canada cost, OPT/CPT timing, campus support |
| `city_life` | Off-campus cost, food, transit, and cheap meals create saveable posts. | NYC under $20/day, campus-area rent comparison |

## Lower-Priority Or Rework Required

Do not rank these highly unless they are reframed:

- Generic admissions administration updates.
- Broad freshman tips without school, situation, or checklist specificity.
- "Top 10 Best Universities" style ranking without a debate, utility, or surprise angle.
- Single-source personal anecdotes with no broader pattern.
- Major regret content that does not connect to data, internships, salary, workload, or decision-making.

Reframe dry topics by explaining what they mean for application strategy, cost, housing, school choice, student segment, or student debate.

## Diversity Rules

- Target at least 12 ranked topics; 15-20 is better when evidence supports it.
- Top 10 should include at least 6 topic lanes where possible.
- Try to include at least 4 of these lanes daily: `unexpected_culture`, `school_specific_utility`, `freshman_transition`, `cost_anxiety`, `identity_belonging`, `debate_ranking`.
- Do not let AI, admissions administration, or one university dominate the list.
- If the base Top 10 includes N AI-related topics, add at least N non-AI topics after rank 10, up to rank 20.

## Content Signals To Extract

For each viable cluster, capture the student audience segment, topic lane, IGOTIN pattern match, hook candidates, share reason, save reason, comment prompt, suggested format, and risk note.
