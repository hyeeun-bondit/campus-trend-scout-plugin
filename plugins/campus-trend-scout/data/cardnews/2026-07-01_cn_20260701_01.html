Breaking Today
As of today, New Jersey City University officially stopped existing as its own school.
  • Its merger into Kean University became fully effective July 1, rebranding the campus as "Kean Jersey City."
  • The state is funding the transition with $25 million — but the deal was signed over a year before most students knew the details.

By the Numbers
8 colleges are closing this year, 6 more are merging, and 2025 saw 16 closures before that.
  • Three of the eight 2026 closures are small Massachusetts liberal arts colleges — Labouré, Hampshire, and Anna Maria.
  • Nearly every closing school saw enrollment drop 30–70% over the last decade.

The Bigger Picture
Huron Consulting projects up to 1 in 4 private nonprofit colleges could close or merge within a decade.
  • Since March 2020, about 48 nonprofit colleges have already closed or announced closing — affecting 52,600+ students.
  • Even Yale is cutting its budget over a new $300M annual tax bill. This isn't just a small-school problem.

If Your School Closes
You have 120 days after an official closure date to qualify for federal loan discharge.
  • If you accept a teach-out transfer to finish your degree elsewhere, you forfeit that discharge option.
  • Most 2026 closures already lined up teach-out partners — check your school's plan before you decide.
Source: U.S. Department of Education rules, via The College Investor
