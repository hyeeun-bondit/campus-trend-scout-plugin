## Today's One-Line Call
Make the "weird real college classes" carousel first: it's the only topic today with zero overlap, zero risk, and a built-in "is this seriously real" hook that travels on its own.

## Make These 3 Today

### 1. Unexpected real college classes
Priority: Very high
Angle: reversal + tag-a-friend
Target: all college students, especially undecided/incoming freshmen browsing major options
Hook: "Is this seriously a real college class?"
Evidence: Cornell offers a tree-climbing course, Pepperdine gives credit for surfing, Penn State runs an ice cream science program — all verified, currently listed courses.
Title candidates: "Wait, this is a real class?" / "Your tuition pays for tree-climbing somewhere" / "5 classes you didn't know existed"
Slides: cover (is this seriously real?) → Cornell tree-climbing → Pepperdine surfing → Penn State ice cream science → one more wildcard → CTA (does your school have one?)
Comment prompt: "Does your school have anything this weird?"
Expected reaction: agree side floods with their own school's weird classes; skeptical side asks for proof — both drive comments.
Caution: keep it celebratory, not mocking — no joke at any specific school's expense.

### 2. Roommate/dorm anxiety, with an actual first-message script
Priority: High
Angle: comfort + checklist
Target: incoming freshmen and sophomores about to room with someone new
Hook: "People who couldn't sleep after their roommate match"
Evidence: multiple College Confidential threads show the same panic from both freshmen and returning sophomores, with users specifically asking what to text first rather than asking for general advice.
Title candidates: "You're not the only one panicking about your roommate" / "What do you even text a stranger you're about to live with?" / "Roommate anxiety, solved in 2 messages"
Slides: cover → why everyone feels this → message template 1 → message template 2 → what actually helped past students → CTA (meet your future roommate on IGOTIN first)
Comment prompt: "What did you actually text your roommate first?"
Expected reaction: high comfort/relate comments, some sharing their own opening message as proof it worked.
Caution: normalize the anxiety, don't frame it as a diagnosable condition — keep tone supportive, not clinical.

### 3. City-by-city student cost comparison (NYC / Bay Area / Toronto)
Priority: High
Angle: comparison + save-for-later
Target: students choosing where to live this fall, especially transfers and incoming students picking apartments
Hook: "Same school year, totally different budgets"
Evidence: a published 2026 cost-of-living guide puts NYC student budgets at roughly $2,500–$5,500/month; Bay Area and Toronto creator content shows comparable rent pressure with different specifics.
Title candidates: "What a month in NYC actually costs a student" / "Bay Area rent will shock you" / "Pick your city, pick your budget"
Slides: cover → NYC monthly breakdown → Bay Area rent reality → Toronto cost overview → one saving tip → CTA (what's your city?)
Comment prompt: "How much does your city actually cost you a month?"
Expected reaction: high save rate, comment thread turns into a crowd-sourced cost comparison.
Caution: cite the guide's time frame in caption since living costs shift fast.
